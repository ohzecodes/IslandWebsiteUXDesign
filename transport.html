Transportation

Getting to Taniti

Airport

Cost: dependent on flight

Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

cruise ship

Cost: dependent on ship fee

Some visitors arrive on a cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.

Getting Around

Walk

Cost: Free

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.

Bicycle

Cost: $10 per hour

Using a bicycle is not a bad choice. Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors helmets are required by law.

Hotel shuttle

Cost: Free

Your Hotel may provide a shuttle for going to the mountains and the rainforest.

Bus

Cost: $20 per person

The bus only goes to the mountains and the rainforest. For in city travel using a public transport will be best.

Car Share

Cost: $4 per hour

Taniti has many local car sharing companies.

rental car

$50 to $400 per day

Taniti has many local car rental companies who provide a variety of cars including luxary, sports and economy.

Helicopter

Cost: $50 per hour

Ideal for long trips

Public Transportation

In-city buses, Taxis and Trains for long distance travel.

Taniti.gov

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. The island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.
